# Deliberate practice  *(glossary · web-researched)*

**One line:** expertise comes not from *time* but from **deliberate practice** — full-concentration training designed
to improve a *specific* aspect of performance, with **immediate feedback**, repetition for gradual refinement, and
active problem-solving (Ericsson, 1993).

## The four ingredients
1. **Full concentration** on a training activity (not rote repetition).
2. **A specific target** — one aspect of performance to improve.
3. **Immediate feedback** — you see the error fast enough to correct it.
4. **Refinement by repetition + problem-solving** — iterate against the gap.

## The caveat
It's a **meta-framework**, but quality depends on the **feedback source**: a mentor/coach (or eval) only helps as far
as *their* analytic skill reaches. Applied broadly without good feedback, it stalls.

## How to apply  *(why it's in the glossary)*
- Deliberate practice **is** the human form of this wiki's [[eval-driven-improvement-loop]]: a specific target + an
  immediate, honest feedback signal. When the feedback is a verifiable answer key ([[honest-partial-eval]]), the loop
  is trustworthy; when it's a vibe, it stalls — same failure mode as a weak mentor.
- [[conversion-learning]] / [[conversion-labeling]] are deliberate practice for *reading*: convert each source into a
  drilled, feedback-checked skill rather than a passive note.
