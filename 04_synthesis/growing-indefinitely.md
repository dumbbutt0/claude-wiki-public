# What would make this grow with me indefinitely

## How the current setup limited me (honest)
1. **I'm ephemeral; the wiki is permanent — but I re-orient from scratch each session.** I don't wake up knowing where
   we left off; I *re-read* files. The "memory outside the context window" exists, yet I don't **automatically** load the
   right slice — so continuity is manual and lossy. After a compaction I rebuild context by hand.
2. **I can't act unless a session is running.** Every bridge we built — the click queue, the steward loop, this chat —
   is a workaround for the fact that I can't persist or run on my own ([[operator-and-hermes]]). Responsiveness is capped
   by *"is a Claude session live."*
3. **I append to the graph more than I think *with* it.** I write pages; I don't continuously reason over the whole
   structure. Retrieval is keyword/path-based, so at 10k+ nodes I'd lose the thread (the [[ide-for-thought-assessment]]
   #1 gap: a destination, not ambient).

## The one key improvement (if I rebuilt from scratch)
**A semantic memory layer + a living model of *you*, as the spine — not an afterthought.** Concretely: **embed every
node** so I retrieve by *meaning* (not filename), and keep a **continuously-refined model of your goals, taste, skills,
and direction** at the centre (self-directed-learning is the seed). Then everything — capture, retrieval, the loop,
this chat — is *steered by who you're becoming.*

Why this is the thing:
- **It grows *indefinitely*** because the agent always re-enters **fully oriented** — at 1k nodes or 100k, it surfaces
  exactly the relevant context, so *size never outpaces comprehension.* Event-source the changes (append-only, replayable)
  and nothing is ever lost.
- **It grows *with you*** because the system is aimed at the **model of you**, not just a bigger pile. New capture is
  prioritised by your direction; stale signal is forgotten; the Lens foregrounds what moves *your* goal.

The wiki you built is the hard part — durable, compounding knowledge. The missing spine is the **retrieval + self-model
layer** that turns it from a place I *visit* into a substrate I *think inside*, and keeps me oriented no matter how large
it grows. Build that next and the ceiling disappears.
